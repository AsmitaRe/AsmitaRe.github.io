An object in the medium experiences buoyant force, the science why plastic ball floats or a ship don't sink. Buoyant force is equal to the weight of displaced fluid/medium. Implies the force is proportional to the product of, the density of the medium $\rho_{m}$, the volume of the bob $V_{b}$, and the acceleration due to Earth's gravity of the Earth $g_{e}$, acting in upward direction.

$F_{b} = \rho_{m}*V_{bob}*g_{e} \hskip 15em$ ..eq(1)

The general equation of motion is,
$\tau = I\alpha = mL^{2}\frac{d^{2}\theta}{dt^{2}} \hskip 15em$ ..eq(2)


Equation of motion of the pendulum with the torque provided by gravitational and buoyant force is,
$$ mL\frac{d^{2}\theta}{dt^{2}} + mg_{e}sin\theta - \rho_{m}V_{bob}g_{e} = 0$$
